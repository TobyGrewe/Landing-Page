A collection of essays exploring geography, culture, art, and music through thoughtful analysis and creative expression.

Nicaragua's Revolution

Political Geography

An exploration of Nicaragua's turbulent political climate, natural disasters, and the resulting migration crisis under Daniel Ortega's authoritarian rule.

"An equally devastating disaster lies in the political system of Nicaragua, a system with a violent political climate of oppression, war, and corruption."

Parks and Recreation

Urban Planning

A thoughtful examination of public parks' role in community well-being, environmental sustainability, and the critical importance of equitable access.

"Parks offer a third place for people to interact with one another, away from work, school, and home."

The Burden to Bear

Literature & Art

A comparative analysis of Naguib Mahfouz's "Palace Walk" and Timothy O'Sullivan's "A Harvest of Death," exploring themes of destiny, freedom, and the human condition.

"The destiny of life and death are the same in the end. The way we live is by the same means in which we die."

Dearly Beloved

Art Analysis

An intimate examination of Frida Kahlo's "The Wounded Deer," exploring themes of pain, identity, and cultural fusion through the lens of the iconic artist's life.

"Her soul is the canvas, and her life is the colors."

It's the Mud

Music & Culture

A journey through the emergence of Bossa Nova in 1950s Brazil, tracing its African roots, cultural significance, and the story behind "Águas de Março."

"Though it may be miserable during this period of terrible rainfall, the rain washes more than mud; it cleanses people of their sorrows."
